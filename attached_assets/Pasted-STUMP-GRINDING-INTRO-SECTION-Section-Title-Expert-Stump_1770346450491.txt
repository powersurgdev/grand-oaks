Stump grinding is a straightforward process, but it requires the right equipment and careful planning to protect your property. Our team ensures every job is handled safely, cleanly, and efficiently from start to finish.

Step Breakdown

Site & Utility Check
We confirm the area is clear and identify any underground utilities before grinding begins.

5. STUMP GRINDING FAQs (Full Copy)
How deep do you grind the stump?

Most stumps are ground approximately 6–12 inches below ground level, which is ideal for covering with soil, planting grass, or completing basic landscaping.

If you have specific plans for the area, we can recommend the right depth during your estimate.

What happens to the wood chips after grinding?

Stump grinding produces natural wood mulch and chips. Many homeowners choose to keep the chips for landscaping, while others prefer removal or backfilling.

We’ll go over the best option for your yard before the job begins.

Can you grind a stump in a fenced backyard?

Yes. Our equipment can access many residential backyards, including properties with standard gate openings.

If access is tight, we’ll evaluate the space and confirm the best approach during your estimate.

Will the stump grow back after grinding?

In most cases, grinding prevents regrowth by removing the stump well below the surface. While certain tree species may attempt minor sprouting, stump grinding is one of the most effective solutions for long-term removal.

We can also discuss additional options if regrowth prevention is a concern.

Do you offer stump grinding as an add-on service?

Yes — stump grinding is a great add-on after tree removal. It’s the best way to fully clear the area, improve curb appeal, and prepare your yard for future landscaping or construction.

Is cleanup included with stump grinding?

Absolutely. We take pride in leaving your property clean and safe once the work is complete. Cleanup is always included, and we’ll make sure the area is ready for whatever comes next.
